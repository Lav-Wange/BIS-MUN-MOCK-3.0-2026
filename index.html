The Legacy of Dialogue

For over a decade, BIS-MUN has hosted forums of rigorous intellectual exchange, raising critical debates to inspire collaborative action.

Fostering the Leaders of Tomorrow

Model United Nations is more than just a simulation; it is an academic incubator that challenges students to step into the shoes of diplomats, understand international relations, and master negotiation.

This year's theme focuses on resilience, exploring how multilateral institutions can adapt to modern challenges such as climate vulnerability, cyber conflict, and global migrations.

Committees of the Assembly

Select an organ to discover its agenda, executive board details, and research direction.

UNHCR
United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees
Single Delegate Intermediate
Director & Chairperson
Sarah Jenkins
Co-Chairperson
Kabir Mehta

Agenda: Addressing Protracted Displacement and Access to Basic Social Services in Conflict Zones

Protracted displacement crises present a fundamental failure of international stabilization networks. UNHCR delegates will address systemic gaps in food security, clean water, schooling, and healthcare integration for refugees trapped in long-standing transit centers or hostile borders.

The committee expects detailed proposals on humanitarian funding allocations, local host community integration projects, and policy adaptations for refugees seeking legal status under modern asylum frameworks.

AIPPM
All India Political Parties Meet
Regional Assembly Advanced
Moderator
Rohan Deshmukh
Deputy Moderator
Ananya Sen

Agenda: Re-evaluating Federal Relations, Regional Disparities, and Economic Redistribution in India

The All India Political Parties Meet is a dynamic forum replicating domestic parliamentary governance. In this session, delegates representing various political alignments will deliberate on complex federal relationships.

Discussions will center on reforming centralized tax pooling, easing the fiscal strain of less developed states, and resolving the political friction between Union mandates and State autonomy. High-stakes negotiations, alliances, and press conferences are expected.

UNSC
United Nations Security Council
Double Delegate Expert / Veto Allowed
President
Marcus Aurelius Thorne
Vice-President
Lin Yang

Agenda: Securing Post-Conflict Transitions: Establishing Frameworks for Peacebuilding and Demilitarized Zones

As the principal organ tasked with international security, the UNSC must craft frameworks to prevent ceasefire decays. Delegates will negotiate parameters of peace monitoring, buffer zone regulation, and demilitarization strategies in active transition states.

This simulation features a fast-paced environment with real-time updates and crisis alerts. Veto power will be active, requiring absolute consensus-building or strategic compromise.

DISEC
Disarmament & International Security
Single Delegate Intermediate
Chairperson
David Sterling
Vice-Chairperson
Fatima Al-Sayed

Agenda: Regulating Autonomous Weapons Systems (AWS) and Cyber-Warfare Frameworks in Modern Geopolitics

The emergence of AI in combat systems poses unprecedented threats to established laws of war. DISEC delegates must define clear definitions of "autonomous lethality" and find consensus on oversight.

Additionally, delegates will attempt to formulate international guidelines on cyber espionage and structural attacks, balancing defense capabilities with global digital infrastructure safety.

Apply for DISEC Background Guide
UNEP
United Nations Environment Programme
Single/Double Beginner Friendly
Director
Dr. Elena Rostova
Rapporteur
Kenji Sato

Agenda: Mitigating the Transnational Impact of Plastic Pollution on Marine Ecosystems and Coastal Vulnerabilities

Plastic pollutants threaten global marine biodiversity and local coastal economies. UNEP delegates are tasked with negotiating a comprehensive international treaty restricting plastic waste exports and funding new research into circular biomaterials.

This committee is designed to welcome delegates of all experience levels, guiding participants through the basics of policy formation and resolution construction.

Registration & Verification

Opening Plenary

Committee Session I

Diplomatic Luncheon

Committee Session II

Substantive debates. Unmoderated caucuses, draft working papers introduction, and position refinement.

Committee Session III

Voting Blocks
